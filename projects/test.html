Menger Sponge

The Menger Sponge is a 3D fractal with infinite surface area but zero volume. It was first described by Austrian mathematician Karl Menger in 1926.

The fractal is created by dividing a cube into 27 smaller cubes, removing the center cube and the six cubes that share a face with it, and then recursively applying this process to the remaining smaller cubes.

Mandelbulb

The Mandelbulb is a three-dimensional analog of the Mandelbrot set, discovered by Daniel White and Paul Nylander in 2009.

It extends the Mandelbrot formula to 3D space using spherical coordinates and trigonometric functions. The power parameter controls the complexity and shape of the fractal.
